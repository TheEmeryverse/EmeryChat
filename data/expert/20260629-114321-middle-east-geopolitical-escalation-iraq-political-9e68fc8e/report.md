# 🌍 Middle East Strategic Brief: Iraq, Hezbollah, Iran & Hormuz (29 June 2026)

## 📋 Executive Summary
The region is currently caught in a synchronized cascade of crises. In **Iraq**, deep factional purges and power grabs are fracturing the Popular Mobilization Forces (PMF), triggering fears of a soft coup or internal regime seizure that rivals traditional military takeovers. The **Israel-Hezbollah** conflict has reverted to active kinetic engagement following the expiration of the November 2024 ceasefire in March 2026. Simultaneously, a fragile **US-Iran MoU** brokered on June 17 at Versailles barely holds as US-Iranian military strikes continue over the Strait of Hormuz. The **Strait of Hormuz** has effectively ceased functioning for commercial shipping since late February, causing a historic energy supply shock and pushing oil prices to $92–$130/bbl [S31][S34].

## 🇮🇶 Iraq: Internal Purge & Factional Power Struggle
Recent reporting—highlighted by analyst Babak Taghvaee regarding a current "coup/purge" in Baghdad [User Input]—corroborates intelligence indicating severe internal restructuring of Iraqi security and political apparatuses.

*   **The "Purge" Dynamics:** The PMF, historically the dominant non-state military force, is experiencing deep internal fractures [S3][S27]. Reports indicate a power consolidation or "purge" targeting dissenting factions within the Shiite militia coalition, effectively functioning as a soft coup by hardline elements to assert control over state institutions and bypass stalled parliamentary transitions.
*   **US & PMF Friction:** The US continues aerial operations against PMF personnel [S30]. Kataib Hezbollah issued a five-day ultimatum for US withdrawal in March 2026, signaling that the "purge" or factional realignment is also driven by an desire to expunge American influence from Iraqi soil [S30].
*   **Political Stalemate:** The November 2025 elections failed to resolve the crisis. Baghdad remains paralyzed by a stalled power transition, allowing militia factions to operate with impunity, targeting Kurdish leadership and US facilities while the central government offers only rhetorical condemnation [S3][S4][S30].
*   **Proxy Integration:** Iraqi militias continue serving as financial and logistical nodes for Iran and Hezbollah via informal fundraising networks [S28], but structural degradation of this proxy model limits their ability to act cohesively [S27].

## 🇱🇧 Israel-Hezbollah: Post-Ceasefire Escalation
The 60-day ceasefire (effective Nov 2024) expired on **2 March 2026** amid mutual allegations of violations. The diplomatic framework has collapsed into active engagement.

*   **Active Kinetic Phase:** Israeli airstrikes have resumed in southern Lebanon under "Operation Closing Verse," targeting Hezbollah infrastructure [S12]. Hezbollah maintains it is exercising self-defense rights against ceasefire violations.
*   **Casualty & Displacement Metrics:** Over **4,200 killed** and **12,186 injured**, with >1.2 million internally displaced [S2][S7]. The Lebanese state's "Homeland Shield Plan" to disarm Hezbollah (approved Aug 2025) is effectively stalled by the resurgence of hostilities [S7].
*   **Diplomatic Deadlock:** A US-led five-country monitoring panel remains nominally active but has lost enforcement capacity. No formal escalation threshold has been met to trigger a broader regional war, though the risk of spillover remains acute.

## 🇮🇷 Iran War & MoU Treaty Status
A temporary **Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)** was signed on **17 June 2026 at the Palace of Versailles**, ostensibly ending hostilities between Iran and Israel [S40]. However, the situation remains volatile.

*   **Ceasefire Fragility:** Described by analysts as "uneasy" and "tenuously holding" [S18]. While active large-scale strikes have paused, US-Iran naval and aerial skirmishes persist over the Strait of Hormuz.
*   **Nuclear Posture:** The IAEA verification regime is severely disrupted following strikes in June 2025 and Feb 2026. ~400kg of 60% enriched uranium remains unaccounted for [S16]. Iran has enriched uranium to near-weapons levels, raising proliferation concerns.
*   **Asymmetric Leverage:** Iran asserts control of the Strait of Hormuz as a strategic veto over global energy flows, using it to negotiate from a position of asymmetric strength against US/Israeli conventional military advantages [S17][S18].

## 🚢 Strait of Hormuz: Commercial Collapse
The Strait has been **economically non-functional** since late February 2026, driven by the withdrawal of war-risk insurance and active Iranian interdiction (vessel seizures/AIS spoofing) [S32].

*   **Volume Crash:** Outbound crude dropped ~95%, LNG ~99%, and fertilizer ~94% from pre-conflict levels by June 2026 [S19][S22]. Inbound agricultural shipments fell 80–85% [S22].
*   **Market Impact:** Brent crude surged to $92–$130/bbl. Global inventories peaked at 8.2 billion barrels [S31][S34]. The US saw a 93.2% YoY drop in imports from affected ports in May 2026 [S33].
*   **Mitigation Failure:** Bypass pipelines (Saudi East-West, UAE ADIP) reroute only 4–7 mb/d against a normal throughput of ~20 mb/d. A residual structural shortage of 7–12 mb/d persists [S31]. Shipping has tentatively recovered to ~54 vessels/day by late June, but volatility remains extreme [S35].

## ⚖️ Competing Interpretations & Uncertainty
*   **Iraq "Coup" vs. Factional Realignment:** While Taghvaee characterizes the events as a coup/purge, analytical sources frame it as deep PMF fracture and hardline consolidation [S3][S27]. The ambiguity lies in whether Baghdad's central government is being bypassed (soft coup) or if the militias are fracturing into competing power centers.
*   **Ceasefire Durability:** The Versailles MoU is viewed as a pause rather than a resolution. Analysts warn that Iran's asymmetric tactics and the degradation of proxy deterrence could lead to renewed escalation if the "uneasy" truce breaks down [S18][S27].
*   **Hormuz Recovery Timeline:** Despite the tentative recovery to 54 vessels/day, war-risk insurance has not been fully restored, and top shipping firms remain risk-averse. A full return to pre-conflict volumes is unlikely before H2 2026 [S32][S35].
*   **Source Reliability Note:** Quantitative data on Hormuz shipping (AIS-tracked) may understate actual traffic due to AIS spoofing/disabling by Iranian authorities [S19][S22]. casualty figures for the Israel-Hezbollah conflict vary between UN, NRC, and Israeli military reports.
