# ICE Buildup in Milwaukee and Waukesha: Research Report

## Executive Summary
Recent activity by Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) in Milwaukee and Waukesha counties indicates a tactical escalation rather than a physical infrastructure buildup. Federal agents have increased visible enforcement operations—including residential detentions, vehicle break-ins, and the separation of asylum seekers—drawing sharp pushback from local municipalities and county officials. Advocacy groups report at least 18 arrests over a single weekend in late June, signaling a targeted surge that contrasts with Milwaukee's established non-cooperation policies [S4][S8].

## Timeline of Events (June 2026)
*   **Late June 2026:** ICE shifts asylum enforcement approach, ending the issuance of notices to appear without specific hearing dates and detaining asylum seekers in separate facilities rather than holding them together for cases [S6].
*   **June 26, 2026:** Advocacy groups document at least four residential detentions in Milwaukee at 23rd St & Greenfield Ave, North 7th St & W. McKinley Place, and 35th St & Clybourn St [S4].
*   **June 28, 2026:** ICE activity reported near Jefferson St & Moreland Rd in Waukesha; agents broke a vehicle window and forced occupants out [S4].
*   **Weekend of June 26–28, 2026:** Federal agents operating unmarked vehicles executed at least 18 arrests across Milwaukee and Waukesha counties [S8].

## Key Actors & Roles
*   **ICE (Federal Agents):** Operating out of unmarked vehicles; executing no-notice residential detentions and enforcing new asylum interpretations. [S3][S6]
*   **Milwaukee Police Department (MPD):** Maintains a policy against cooperating with ICE civil enforcement but has publicly acknowledged lacking a specific contingency plan for mass federal operations [S2][S3].
*   **Milwaukee Aldermen & City Officials:** Drafting ordinances requiring unmasked ICE agents and banning raids on public facilities; expressing alarm over the surge [S5][S8].
*   **Milwaukee County Board of Supervisors:** Adopted a formal resolution opposing ICE presence in courthouses, citing due process violations and community erosion [S13].
*   **Advocacy Groups (Voces de la Frontera, Forward Latino):** Monitoring operations via tip lines; issuing public alerts and providing legal support to affected families [S4][S8].

## Core Findings
1.  **Tactical Escalation Over Infrastructure:** No physical personnel increases or facility expansions have been verified beyond field office activity. The "buildup" consists of heightened enforcement tactics, including unmasked agents and vehicle break-ins at private residences [S1][P5].
2.  **Targeted Residential Detentions:** ICE is conducting active residential arrests in established Milwaukee neighborhoods and Waukesha streets, bypassing traditional processing centers for immediate removal [S4].
3.  **Municipal Pushback:** Both city and county governments are actively resisting enforcement via legislative measures—ordinances requiring agent visibility and resolutions opposing courthouse activity—to protect community trust [S5][S13].

## Competing Interpretations
*   **ICE's Stance (Source S7):** Emphasizes targeting of criminal aliens and violent offenders to prioritize public safety, supported by national initiatives like the VOICE Office and 287(g) partnerships. *(Note: Official source; inherently promotional.)*
*   **Advocacy & Local Official Stance (Source S1, S8):** Disputes ICE's targeting narrative based on detainee demographics. Highlights the intimidation caused by unmasked federal agents operating in residential areas and the erosion of municipal authority [S1][S8].
*   **Law Enforcement Ambiguity (Source S3):** MPD maintains a non-cooperation stance but fails to provide a concrete operational plan for mass ICE surges, creating uncertainty regarding law enforcement's actual response during active federal operations [S3].

## Uncertainty & Confidence Notes
*   **Reliance on Advocacy Reporting:** Detailed tracking of detentions relies heavily on advocacy tip lines and social media reporting rather than independent official verification. *(Note: Moderate reliability based on corroboration by local news outlets such as WTMJ and CBS58)* [S1][S4].
*   **Operational Intent Unverified:** While a localized surge is documented, the underlying strategic rationale (e.g., specific executive orders) remains unconfirmed in available sources. ICE's claims regarding "violent offender" targeting are statistically challenged by local representatives [S2][P2][S1].
*   **Lack of Personnel Data:** Independent staffing or facility expansion data is unavailable; confirmed activity points exclusively to operational/tactical escalation at existing sites [P5].
