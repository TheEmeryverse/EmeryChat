[S3] WPA Slave Narrative Collection - Wikipedia
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WPA_Slave_Narrative_Collection
The WPA Slave Narrative Collection is a government‑funded oral‑history project (1936‑1938) that recorded over 2,000 interviews with formerly enslaved individuals, later published in multiple volumes. It was created by the Federal Writers’ Project under the New Deal and draws on earlier folklore collections. The narratives provide first‑hand accounts of post‑emancipation life, including family stability, crime rates, drug use, and community segregation, but were compiled by mostly white interviewers, raising concerns about bias and selective preservation.

[S19] Works Progress Administration - Wikipedia
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Works_Progress_Administration
The Works Progress Administration (WPA) was a U.S. New Deal agency established on May 6 1935, employing up to 8.5 million people during the Great Depression. It constructed extensive public infrastructure—including roads, bridges, schools, and parks—and provided paid jobs in construction, the arts, and other sectors, aiming to supply one paid job per family in need. Its Federal Project Number One interviewed former slaves, preserving records of African‑American experiences post‑emancipation. The agency was liquidated in June 1943 due to low unemployment during World War II.
